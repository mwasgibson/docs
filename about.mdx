Gibson Muiruri Mwangi is a Software Engineering student who began his degree in 2022 with a clear goal: to build technology that solves real problems. His focus spans artificial intelligence, machine learning, cybersecurity, and research-driven software development. Rather than staying in any single lane, Gibson actively works at the intersection of these fields — designing AI systems with security in mind, grounding his applications in formal research, and writing software that is both intellectually rigorous and practically deployable.

## Who He Is

Gibson approaches engineering as a discipline of thinking as much as building. He is drawn to problems that most people consider too complex or too open-ended — public health surveillance using social media signals, privacy-preserving voice assistants, identity verification systems, and IoT security frameworks. His work is characterized by a research-first mentality: before writing a line of code, he asks what the problem actually is, who it affects, and what constraints the solution must respect. This makes his projects lean, purposeful, and technically honest.

He describes his philosophy simply: good software is built at the intersection of curiosity, craft, and accountability. He enjoys the challenge of translating ideas that exist only as research papers or rough concepts into tools that work reliably in the real world.
