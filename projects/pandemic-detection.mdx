During the early stages of a disease outbreak, social media often surfaces signals — symptom reports, location-tagged illness posts, spikes in health-related queries — days or weeks before official surveillance systems register a case cluster. Gibson Muiruri Mwangi is building a machine learning pipeline that ingests and analyses social media data to provide early-warning signals of potential pandemic events, giving public health stakeholders more time to respond. The project is currently in **Development Phase**, with model architecture underway.

## How It Works

The system processes raw social data through a multi-stage pipeline before producing an actionable outbreak signal:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Social Media Data Ingestion">
    Public posts, threads, and health-related content are collected from social platforms via APIs or scraping pipelines, filtered by language, geography, and topic relevance.
  </Step>
  <Step title="NLP Preprocessing">
    Raw text is cleaned, tokenised, and normalised. Named entity recognition extracts disease names, symptoms, and locations. Noise, spam, and irrelevant content are filtered out.
  </Step>
  <Step title="ML Classification">
    A trained classifier categorises each processed post by disease signal type — symptom report, confirmed case mention, healthcare query, or false positive — generating a structured event stream.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Outbreak Signal Detection">
    Aggregated signals are analysed for temporal and geographic clustering. Anomaly detection identifies sudden spikes that exceed baseline thresholds and flags them as potential outbreak events.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Technical Approach

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="NLP & Text Classification" icon="language">
    Natural language processing models fine-tuned on health-domain text classify posts by symptom type, severity language, and geographic reference.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Social Data Ingestion Pipeline" icon="database">
    A robust ingestion layer collects, deduplicates, and normalises high-volume social media streams for downstream ML processing.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Anomaly & Trend Detection" icon="chart-line">
    Statistical and ML-based anomaly detection identifies abnormal spikes in disease-signal volume across time windows and geographic regions.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Public Health Signal Mapping" icon="map-location-dot">
    Detected signals are mapped to geographic regions and severity tiers, producing structured alerts consumable by public health monitoring systems.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
