## Why He Writes

Technical writing is how Gibson consolidates understanding. Explaining a system clearly enough for another engineer to follow forces a level of precision that is often absent in the act of building — edge cases surface, assumptions become explicit, and architectural weaknesses become visible on the page before they become bugs in production.

Beyond the personal benefit, Gibson writes because the developer community runs on shared knowledge. The same articles, tutorials, and documentation that helped him get started are the kind of resource he aims to contribute back. A well-written technical article can save a dozen other engineers the same hours of debugging and dead-ends — that compounds in a way that individual code contributions rarely do.

His writing spans the full range of his technical interests: AI system architecture, cybersecurity implementation, and the software engineering craft that underpins both. Taken together, the articles are a public record of his thinking as a practitioner — transparent, grounded, and oriented toward what is actually useful.
